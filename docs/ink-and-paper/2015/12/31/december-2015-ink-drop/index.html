The Goulet Pens December 2015 Ink drop finally arrived today.

Inks

Dec Ink Drop All Colours
full size


click on image for slideshow

The December 2015 Ink Drop brought five more colours to the fore.

These colours are clearly selected with the Christmas holiday season in mind, with such names as “Myrrh”, “Frankencense”, “Gold”, and the two tagalong colours for Christmas of “Vibrant Green” and “Bright Red”.

It is a pretty set, and they all display well with the glass pen. Less so with the Q-tip swab, but I blame that more on the swab and the swabber.

I cannot seem to keep the correct spelling for De Atramentis in my head, so there are some creative spellings in the list.

Dec Ink Drop Caran Dache Vibrant Green
full size


click on image for slideshow

I like this green, it’s quite pretty. I would not say so much vibrant from the swab, but from the glass pen, really nice.

Dec Ink Drop De Atramentis Gold
full size


click on image for slideshow

The D.A. gold is pretty nice, too. I like the colour from the pen.

Dec Ink Drop De Atramentis Myrrh
full size


click on image for slideshow

So this blue is nice. “Myrrh” is one of those things most of us go “what the heck is myrrh anyway” so the blue is somewhat of a pleasant surprise. I had no expectations, but I do like it.

Like the D.A. Gold, it’s very nice colour from the pen. It bears a good test on real fountain pen quality paper.

Dec Ink Drop Pelikan Bright Red
full size


click on image for slideshow

Pelikan’s Bright Red is definitely bright. In the swab test it comes out rather coral in colour, but with the glass pen and on the S-B paper below, it’s a very nice bright ink colour.

Dec Ink Drop De Atramentis Frankincense
full size


click on image for slideshow

I think this colour was even more surprising than the Myrrh, and Frankincense also has the mysterious “what the heck is it?” quality about it. The shade in the swab feels a deeper grey than some others, while the glass pen gives a nice enough black.

Dec Ink Drop All Sketchbook
full size


click on image for slideshow

I was not too happy with the swabs of ink on the standard 3x5 cards, so I decided to give them a go on my favourite sketchbook from Stillman & Birn, with their Beta paper.

The results were much more saturated and vibrant, and I gave them a much better amount of ink. They are quite lovely.
