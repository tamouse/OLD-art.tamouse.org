I bought a couple of 5-packs of J. Herbin ink on Massdrop recently, and they arrived today. A mass ink test proceeds!

Interesting Packaging
full size


click on image for slideshow

When I opened up the box, I was really surprised and interested in how the packaged the bottles for protection.

Everything was really well padded. But there were these inflatable bags each protecting two boxes. They looked quite formidable.

Mars Rover Lander Protection

These things look like they’d be good for landing on Mars… if you could use ink on Mars.

I ordered two sets of the 5 ink J. Herbin packages, greedy mouse that I am.

Ink Test - Larmes De Cassis, Lie De The, Vert Empire, Gris Nuage
full size


click on image for slideshow

I did pretty much the same sort of test for all the inks.

  • Larme de Cassis, a really lovely purple. I like this one a lot.
  • Lie de Thé, an equally lovely brown / sepia. This is also really nice.
  • Vert Empire, a rich Imperial green.
  • Gris Nuage, a somewhat pale grey, that actually darkened a bit when it dried down, unexpectedly.

Ink Test - Rouge Caroubiet, Orange Indien, Perle Noire, Terre De Feu
full size


click on image for slideshow

  • Rouge Caroubiet. I find this going off to pink a bit more than I’d like, actually.
  • Orange Indien is a rather nice orange, a colour I’m not overly fond of. This one is rather nice.
  • Perle Noire is a nice black, fairly dark. I definitely like it.
  • Terre de Fue. I really like this one, a sort of brick brown.

Ink Test - Violette Pensee, Rouge Opera
full size


click on image for slideshow

Last two

  • Violette Pensée - this is a purple I really like.
  • Rouge Opéra is a red that goes a bit blue. I like this red a lot, as well.

Ink Test - Abstract With Three Colours
full size


click on image for slideshow

I tried out playing around on a page of S&B Beta paper (last in the book!) and did some blending with water and dry. I really like the way this plays out. The words are complete gibberish, don’t try to figure what they are! Haha.
