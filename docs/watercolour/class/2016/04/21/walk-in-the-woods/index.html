Walk in the Woods

  1. Apr 21, 2016
  2. Categories:
  3. watercolour
  4. class
  5. Tags:
  6. editing
  7. composition
  8. focal-point
  9. WIP

Update 4/21: Finished the painting by giving the figures some shadow to stand upon, and gave some of the trees a bit of gouache.

Update: Second week in class on this painting. I sort of fumbled on the dog, which turned into a large fluffy cat-leopard, sorta…

Last week in class, we started a new painting, of a man walking his dog in the woods. Our task was to “edit” the picture, choosing our own focal point for the image. I’m liking what I’ve done so far. This is still very much a work in progress.

Glazing With Quin Gold
full size


click on image for slideshow

I glazed over the open area on the left, under the woods and next to the large tree on the left in order to give the area a bit of a glow. The space was looking a bit too dull, and I wanted to lift it up a bit before giving it it’s final colour.

Figures With Path Defined
full size


click on image for slideshow

After the quin gold glazing was dry, I gave the left side meadow it’s final colour, a muted green, in order to define the path the figures are walking along.

I filled in the colour on the figures as well. I didn’t want them to much more than gestures, not a lot of details, really. The dog was pretty hard, actually, since it’s walking away from the viewer, all you really have to work with is dog butt. It took me about 4 or 5 tries, at one point it just looked more like a leopard than anything else. This is the best I could do for now.
