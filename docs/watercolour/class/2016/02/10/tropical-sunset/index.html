Tropical Sunset

  1. Feb 10, 2016
  2. Categories:
  3. watercolour
  4. class
  5. Tags:
  6. sunset
  7. beach
  8. tropics
  9. warm

I’m entitling this “Wish I Were There”, I think

Steps

Sky and source image
full size


click on image for slideshow

With the source image behind, I started with the sky. Putting the paper upside-down, I used washes of aureolin, permanent alizerin crimson, and french ultramarine, leaving the disc of the sun paper white.

Sky Closeup

click on image for slideshow

Close up of same sky section.

Water
full size


click on image for slideshow

Flipping the block back upright, I then painted in the water capturing the sky’s reflection and deepening into the blue water closer to the viewer.

Water
full size


click on image for slideshow

Standing up view of the same state.

Deepening the Water
full size


click on image for slideshow

I painted a second layer on the water area to deepen the hues. For some odd reason, the blue water layer refused to darken the way I wanted it to here.

Darken Water
full size


click on image for slideshow

I really darkened the water, using less water on the brush, making sure my paint was well-mixed. I think it worked this time.

Dark Enough
full size


click on image for slideshow

This is after the deeper blue application of paint dried. I spent a lot of time on this painting waiting for things to dry!

Beach Trees Turtles
full size


click on image for slideshow

This is now 2 weeks later, and I’ve put in the far beach with it’s trees, and put in the turtuels. I initially used a putty knife to scrape the water ripples into the tree trunks. I scraped the fuzzy palm leaves with the angled back end of one of my paint brushes.

The sun’s edge was softened a bit.

The turtles were painted using phthalo green, keeping it fairly lightly tinted so not to overpower things.

Finally, I applied some white inktense pencil to give the water more of a ripply effect.
