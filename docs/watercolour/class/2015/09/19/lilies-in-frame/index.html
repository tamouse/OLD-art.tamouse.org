Lilies in Frame

  1. Sep 19, 2015
  2. Categories:
  3. watercolour
  4. class
  5. Tags:
  6. watercolour
  7. class
  8. flowers

This is another try at the technique first explored in flowers in a frame and I think I did a better job with it this time. The author of the book we were working from is Karlyn Holman.

Traced out the major flowers using a photocopy and blue tracing paper.

base colour

I used a combination of sap green, aureolin, a little french ultramarine, and a little quin burnt orange for this. One of the class participants made the remark that it was basically dirty water. I’m working at keeping it pale and light.

stamens
full size


click on image for slideshow

Going a little darker on some of the leaves and bringing out a bit more in the petals.

The stamens are a very bright orangish colour, I don’t quite remember the colours I used, but I think they were quin burnt orange and cobalt red.

taped border
full size


click on image for slideshow

Taped off the border for the backgound window.

masked flowers
full size


click on image for slideshow

It’s harder to see here, but the flowers within the masking tape are covered in masking fluid to protect them.

masked flowers, 2
full size


click on image for slideshow

This view shows the masking fluid (drying down) a little better.

The basic idea is to leave the area of the window that will get the backgound colouring open but leave the flowers their delicate shades.

background applied
full size


click on image for slideshow

The beauty of this method is apparent here, where I can just slop on the colour and let it move and play while wet, adding different paint to mix on the paper directly.

The primary colour is, of course, our favourite, quin burnt orange. Is there nothing this colour cannot do? Additionally mixed in some french ultramarine and probably a couple other things.

touching up
full size


click on image for slideshow

After the background dried, and the masking fluid and tape removed, there remain a few touch ups.

Filling in the white parts next to leaves, stems, and odd bits here an there, and applying a little coloured pencil to acheive a little more texture in some areas.

Materials

Paper

  • Arches 9x12 140lb cold press block

Paints

  • WN Sap Green
  • DS Aureolin (Cobalt Yellow)
  • DS Quin Burnt Orange
  • WN French Ultramarine Blue
  • WN Permanent Alizerin Crimson
  • DS Phthalo Green

Brushes

  • Dreamcatcher #10 round
