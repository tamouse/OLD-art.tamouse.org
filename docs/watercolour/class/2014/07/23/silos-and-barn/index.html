Silos and Barn

  1. Jul 23, 2014
  2. Categories:
  3. watercolour
  4. class
  5. Tags:
  6. watercolour
  7. class

Interesting subject, and the work here was to get a good sense of using shading to make the silos round.

Steps

Original Subject
full size


click on image for slideshow

A photo found by our teacher for us to copy.

Negative Painting Foliage Define Buildings
full size


click on image for slideshow

The vegetation created a negative space around the buildings, that will give the buildings definition.

Shading On Sides
full size


click on image for slideshow

Start in on shading the side of the barn. The light is coming from behind and to the right of the subjects. It’s bright, direct light, and creates sharp defined shadows.

Deeper Shading
full size


click on image for slideshow

Continue deepening the shading on the buildings.

Very Deep Shadows Giving Roundness
full size


click on image for slideshow

The shading needs to be deepest for the silos at the left edge and taper towards the center, giving the round silo it’s essence.

Edging Details
full size


click on image for slideshow

Give some the smaller pipes some better shape and letting them stick out from the buildings better.

More Edging Details
full size


click on image for slideshow

Add more edging to the details

Rust, Windows, and Sign
full size


click on image for slideshow

One of the neat characteristics of the silos, in particular, is the rust on the buildings, and the character that adds to them.

The windows had some interesting details happening. I had painted in the glass area in yellow originally, to give an underpainting for them, which will glow through the window details.

The sign got painted in as well, just leaving a bit of “calligraphy” untouched.
